Throughout the final group project for ICS314, we were required to track our effort estimates. This was split up into coding effort and non coding effort. Examples of non coding effort could be design planning, research, collaboration, communication, etc. Some tasks can vary in what kind of effort was required to complete it. To make my own effort estimates, I did my best to guess how much time it would take based on the difficulty level. This means that if there was a more intricate problem, I increased coding and non coding estimates. The reasoning behind this is that I would naturally take more time to code something that I am unfamiliar with, and because I am new to it, I may spend more time researching beforehand. During the issue, I would keep open communication with my team mates so that there are no misunderstandings with completion time. After, I may have errors, merging issues, etc. so it is important to me to fix these issues right away, before it hinders one of my group members. 
Estimating in advance may have helped our group Manoa Study Spaces to delegate work better. If there is an issue that may take a long time for someone we create sub-issues and try to help that group member out. Another benefit to doing effort estimations is realizing what skills that you have acquired as a 314 student. Tracking actual effort was useful in a few ways. It let us know how much time each team member was spending on their part of the project. It also helped us better gauge how to improve our effort estimation in the future. If an issue was too difficult to edit or work on based on previous experience, we may rethink if we want to further it or work on a different part of the project instead. 
To track my actual effort, I used Microsoft clock. I think that my tracking ended up being fairly accurate with my estimates. Though, if I had the chance to do it again, I would be more accurate with my start and stop times. In between, I tended to mix the coding and non coding times, which made it hard to be very precise with the difference. I would also be more organized with the way that I track my times throughout the day and across multiple days by putting it into a sheet.
